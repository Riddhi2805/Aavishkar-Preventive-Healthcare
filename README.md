# 🩺 Breaking Barriers: Making Preventive Healthcare Affordable

## Overview

This research project was conducted under the Aavishkar Research Competition and focused on understanding the factors influencing participation in preventive healthcare check-ups.

The study examined how healthcare costs, income levels, awareness, and accessibility affect an individual's willingness to undergo preventive health screenings. Using survey-based statistical analysis, the project identified key barriers to preventive healthcare adoption and proposed a technology-driven solution to improve accessibility and engagement.

The findings from this research later inspired the development of **HealthGuard AI**, an AI-powered preventive healthcare application.

---

## Research Objective

The primary objectives of this study were:

* To analyze public awareness regarding preventive healthcare.
* To examine the impact of healthcare costs on participation in preventive check-ups.
* To investigate the relationship between income and healthcare screening frequency.
* To identify barriers preventing individuals from seeking preventive healthcare.
* To propose practical and technology-driven solutions for improving healthcare accessibility.

---

## Data Collection

### Survey Methodology

* Survey Type: Structured Questionnaire
* Sampling Technique: Stratified Random Sampling
* Sample Size: 370 Respondents
* Study Area: Mumbai, India

The questionnaire collected information related to:

* Age Group
* Income Level
* Healthcare Spending
* Frequency of Preventive Check-Ups
* Awareness of Preventive Healthcare
* Cost Sensitivity
* Willingness to Participate in Health Screenings

---

## Statistical Analysis

The following statistical techniques were applied:

### 1. One-Sample Proportion Test

Used to determine whether the proportion of respondents willing to participate in preventive healthcare programs was significantly different from the hypothesized value.

### 2. Chi-Square Test of Independence

Used to examine the association between healthcare costs and frequency of preventive health check-ups.

### 3. Pearson Correlation Analysis

Used to measure the relationship between income level and frequency of preventive healthcare participation.

---

## Key Findings

### Preventive Healthcare Participation

* Approximately 64.4% of respondents expressed willingness to participate in preventive healthcare initiatives.
* Statistical testing confirmed that preventive healthcare interest exists among the surveyed population.

### Cost as a Barrier

* The Chi-Square Test revealed a significant relationship between healthcare costs and screening frequency.
* Higher healthcare costs were associated with lower participation in preventive check-ups.

### Income and Screening Frequency

* Pearson Correlation Analysis showed a positive relationship between income and preventive healthcare participation.
* Individuals with higher income levels were more likely to undergo regular preventive screenings.

### Awareness Gap

* Many respondents demonstrated limited awareness regarding the long-term benefits of preventive healthcare.
* Lack of information and affordability concerns were identified as major barriers.

## Proposed Solution

Based on the findings, a technology-driven healthcare platform was proposed to improve preventive healthcare participation.

Proposed features included:

* Preventive Health Check-Up Booking
* Healthcare Cost Comparison
* Laboratory and Hospital Discovery
* AI-Based Medical Report Summarization
* Personalized Health Recommendations
* Health Screening Reminders
* Appointment Management
* Preventive Healthcare Awareness Resources

This concept later evolved into the HealthGuard AI application.
